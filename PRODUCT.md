# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fauna is for local-AI builders, developers, and power users working in a browser-based tool while running models and generation services on their own machine. They use it to prompt local Ollama models, attach files, explore web-grounded answers, preview generated code, and experiment with image or video generation without leaving the local workspace.

## Product Purpose

Fauna exists to make local AI feel capable, polished, and practical. Success means a user can move from prompt to useful output quickly, understand which local model or tool is active, recover gracefully when a local service is missing, and trust the interface during repeated creative or technical workflows.

## Brand Personality

Local, premium, calm. The product should feel self-contained and serious without becoming cold: a focused local workstation for AI experimentation with a restrained, high-quality English interface voice.

## Anti-references

Fauna should not feel like a generic SaaS landing page, a noisy AI demo, a glassy gradient chatbot clone, or a cluttered enterprise dashboard. Avoid decorative effects that compete with the task, inconsistent control styling, over-bright inactive states, and mixed-language copy.

## Design Principles

- Local-first confidence: make local services, model choices, and failure states legible without drama.
- Calm density: keep workflows efficient and scannable while avoiding visual noise.
- Premium restraint: use polish, spacing, contrast, and consistency instead of flashy decoration.
- Tool clarity: every control should reveal what it does, what state it is in, and what happens next.
- English-only clarity: user-facing copy should be concise, consistent, and fully English.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls, support reduced-motion preferences, preserve keyboard access for core chat and settings workflows, and avoid relying on color alone for state. Interface copy should be maintained in English with clear, consistent terms for shared controls and status messages.
